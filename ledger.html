Leang Karampuang: narrative scene of figures interacting with a pig. The composition strongly implies shared storytelling conventions within the producing cohort, but “semantic continuity” as defined in the protocol requires meaning-system persistence across generations. A single painted scene, even if narrative, does not establish this. Downgraded from PROBABLE-[Continuity] to UNDERDETERMINED in v0.3.

Liang Metanduno (Muna Island): hand stencil dated ≥67,800 ya. Distinct site, distinct motif from Karampuang. Not a re-dating but a new minimum-age constraint.

Semantic Discriminant Condition: Identification of recurring narrative motifs across temporally separated panels (>40 layers apart) within the Sulawesi tradition. Feasibility: MODERATE (further LA-U-series dating under way across multiple cave sites).

Rupture risk: Probable discontinuity ~20,000–4,000 ya (LGM sea-level changes to Austronesian arrival). Practice continuity across this gap is undemonstrated.

Cave bear femur with holes interpreted by excavators (Turk et al.) as a Neanderthal flute. Taphonomic critique (Chase & Nowell; d’Errico et al.) argues holes are consistent with carnivore gnawing. Technical analyses by both camps remain in direct conflict. The bone itself is uncontested; its status as an instrument is the disputed claim.

Material-as-instrument Discriminant Condition: Independent replication study demonstrating that the hole pattern cannot be produced by cave hyena gnawing under controlled conditions, OR demonstration that it can. Feasibility: MODERATE (experimental taphonomy is feasible but has not resolved the dispute after 25+ years of debate).

D — Category-Level Observations

D1 · Body Ornamentation (Global)

Species-wide capacity continuity. Humans have never abandoned ornamental behaviour once established (~142,000 ya → present). Scored as category, not tradition. Individual ornamental traditions (Bizmoune beads, Blombos beads, Skhul/Qafzeh beads) are scored separately as traditions.

D2 · Mortuary Practice (Global)

Species-wide capacity continuity. Intentional burial from at least ~100,000 ya (Qafzeh, Skhul) through to present. Possibly Neanderthal from ~60,000+ ya (Shanidar, La Chapelle-aux-Saints). Scored as category, not tradition.

D3 · Music-Making (Global)

Species-wide capacity continuity from at least ~43,000 ya (Swabian Jura flutes) → present. Individual musical instrument traditions (Swabian flutes, Jiahu flutes) are scored separately. The Divje Babe bone would extend this to ~54,000 ya if confirmed as instrument.
