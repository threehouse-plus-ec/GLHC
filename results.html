Q1 — How Deep Is the Chain?

Answer: From the conventional anchor at 52,500 ya, approximately N ≈ 1,750–2,100 parent layers separate us from the earliest securely dated figurative art tradition. From the deepest ornamental evidence (Bizmoune, ~142,000 ya), the chain extends to N ≈ 4,730–5,680.

Q2 — Which Traditions Cross the Threshold?

Answer: All assessed traditions with securely dated material evidence exceed N > 40 by large margins. The threshold discriminates tradition from fashion but does not create close calls in the current compilation. The real discriminant is not whether traditions cross the threshold, but at which levels (Material, Practice, Semantic) and through which channels (Strat, Cust) they can be confirmed.

Key Finding: Australian Convergence

The Australian corridor (Madjedbebe → Murujuga → Nawarla Gabarnmang → Kimberley) is unique in the global compilation for three convergent reasons:

  1. Depth: Material evidence spans ≥65,000 years (~2,416 layers), the deepest continuous occupation sequence outside Africa.
  2. Dual-channel confirmation: Stratigraphic and custodial evidence converge independently, with Nawarla Gabarnmang achieving CONFIRMED at all three levels through both channels.
  3. Living custodianship: Aboriginal communities maintain active connection to these sites — not as heritage objects but as ongoing cultural practice with internal accountability structures.

Candidate comparative claim (not yet formalised): Aboriginal Australian landscape-anchored creative traditions represent the longest documented continuous creative transmission chains. This claim requires a full Ledger entry (CL-2026-XXX) with explicit competitors and discriminant conditions before it can be promoted from finding-in-waiting to formal result.

Error Budget

The following table ranks uncertainty sources by their contribution to overall assessment confidence. Sources are ordered from largest to smallest impact.

EB-1 · g variance ±17% on N All layer-depth calculations Sensitivity band (g=25–30) applied to all entries. No threshold-crossings in current compilation are sensitive to this range. Sex-specific g noted for lineage-specific chains.
EB-2 · Epistemic asymmetry Binary (present/absent) All non-Australian sites Dual-channel protocol. Where custodial evidence is absent, scored “—”. Where present, convergence with stratigraphic evidence raises confidence. Custodial Null defined for explicit disavowal vs. simple absence.
EB-3 · Cascade rule Systematic downgrade All Semantic scores without Practice anchor Explicitly epistemic, not ontological. Prevents over-claiming at Level 3. Leang Karampuang Semantic downgraded to UNDERDETERMINED in v0.3 under this rule.

EB-5 · Pedigree collapse Systematic (depth ≠ breadth) Interpretation of N N is a depth metric, not a count of distinct ancestors. Strengthened with palaeogenomic turnover evidence (Bergström et al., Lazaridis et al.).
EB-6 · Neanderthal boundary Binary (species scope) El Castillo, Divje Babe Treated separately. If Neanderthal authorship confirmed, “humankind” scope must be redefined. Deferred to separate deliberation (Guardian flag #5).
EB-7 · Convergence vs. diffusion Classificatory Cross-continental comparisons PROBABLE-[Convergent] annotation distinguishes independent invention from transmission-based continuity. Sulawesi shows no plausible diffusion route from Africa.
EB-8 · UNDERDETERMINED feasibility Variable (LOW to HIGH) Resolution of open entries Every UNDERDETERMINED entry carries a Discriminant Condition and Feasibility Flag. Many deep-time entries have LOW feasibility (no living tradition, no ethnographic parallels).
EB-9 · Stratigraphic gaps Site-specific Continuity claims at individual sites Nawarla: 28 ka pictograph to post-1440 CE art — connection partly inferential, bridged by custodial evidence. Sulawesi: probable discontinuity ~20,000–4,000 ya (LGM to Austronesian arrival).
EB-10 · Anchor convention Systematic offset Absolute N-values (not relative comparisons) 52,500 ya is declared convention with Endorsement Marker. Substitution invited. Does not affect inter-site comparisons or threshold-crossing judgments.

Error-budget hierarchy: EB-1 (g variance) and EB-2 (epistemic asymmetry) dominate. EB-1 is a parametric uncertainty that shifts all N-values proportionally but does not affect threshold-crossings. EB-2 is a structural limitation: without custodial evidence, Semantic scores for non-Australian sites are systematically capped at UNDERDETERMINED or PROBABLE. Together, these two sources account for most of the unresolved uncertainty in the compilation.
